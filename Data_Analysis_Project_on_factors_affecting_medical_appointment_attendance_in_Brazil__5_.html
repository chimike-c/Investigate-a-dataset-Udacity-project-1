Introduction¶

In this project, a dataset containing records for about 100k patients in Brazil (May 2016) was analyzed gathered and a few inferences were drawn from the findings.

The dataset includes includes information on medical appointments for patients, showing the date of appointment, neighborhood of patients, medical plan(scholarship), etc. The collected data can be used to infer factors that affect if a patient will attend his/her medical appointment.

The following question statements are posed from information in the selected dataset;

-Does having a welfare program(scholarship) affect the tendency of a patient to show up for their appointment?

-Does the neighborhood of a patient affect the tendency of a patient to show up for their appointment?

-Does a follow up sms notification affect the tendency of a patient to show up for their appointment?

-How does nature of the patient affliction and age range of patinets affect the tendency of a patient to show up for their appointment?

Various correlations will be investigated and tentative conclusions were drawn from the analysis.

Data Wrangling¶

General Properties¶

In this section, the dataset is inspected and trimmed/cleaned before being used for analysis. The steps being used are documnented below.

The the top rows of noshow appointment dataset are displayed below, showing data categorized under 14 columns. The various columns show the following;

Patinet ID - This is a unique number for each patient registered at the hospital.

Appointment ID - This is a unique number that serves as an appointment identifier in the records system.

Gender - This shows the gender of each patient. 'M' for males and 'F' for females.

ScheduledDay - This shows the date the appointment was schduled.

AppointmentDay - This shows the date of the appointment with the Doctor.

Age - This shows the age of the patient.

Neighborhood - This shows the neighborhood of the patient's address.

Scholarship - This indicates if the patient is signed up for a welfare program in 1s and 0s. '1' indicates yes and '0' no.

Hipertension - This shows if a patient is suffers from hypertension.

Diabetes - This shows if a patient is suffers from Diabetes.

Alcoholism - This shows if a patient is suffers from Alcoholism.

Handicap - This shows if a patient is Handicap.

SMS_received - This shows if the patient received an SMS notofication for the appointment in 1s and 0s.

No-show - This shows if a patient showed up for their appointment , Yes or No. 'No' indicates the patient showed up and 'Yes' indicates the patient did not show up.

Conclusions¶

From the data analysis operations carried out, the following tentative points were observed. Also, noting various limitations during the analysis process;

  • Patients being signed up for the welfare program (having scholarships) barely affected the tendency of patients to show up for appointments. Noting that there are relatively few patients signed up for schaolariships in the dataset.

  • The data analysis shows that alot of the patients(more than 60%) that showed up for their appointments received SMS notifications. However it also shows that alot of the patients (more than 75%) that did not show up also received SMS notifications. Therefore there is not a strong correlation between receiving and SMS and patient attending their appointment.

  • A correlation between various neighbourhoods and if patients showed up for their appointments was undeterminable as there were too many neighboorhoods, which are all unrelated (distance from the location of appointment and/or distance from each other) and little to no added informatiom about each neighboorhood.

  • Also as observed, the nature of affliction for each patient i.e Hypertension, Diabetes, Alcoholism, Handicap, does not really affect the tendency of patients to show up for their appointments. In the case of hypertensive patients, about 35% did not show up for their appointments. In the case of diabetic patients, about 35% did not show up for their appointments. In the case of alcoholic patients, about 35% also did not show up for their appointments. In the case of handicap patients, of the number of patients with the various conditions 50% or less did not show up for their apoointments.

The above tentative points can be concluded from the data analysis operation carried out on the selected dataset.
